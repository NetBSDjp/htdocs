I am the maintainer of NetBSD/mac68k, and a former member of the NetBSD core group.

Things that I'm working on now, or intend to shortly:

  • Bring memory mapping in line with other 680x0-based ports as much as is practical, to ease maintenance. This primarily means that I need to figure out how to disable the MMU early on.
  • Acquire significant SCSI clue and fix the machine-independent 5380 driver so that it's reliable for MO, tapes, and other slow SCSI devices.

Things that other people have volunteered to do:

  • Work on a NetBSD-based install process.
  • Fix floating point emulation for the 68LC040-based machines. In particular, FMOVEM emulation is broken, and the Linux/68k fix is a gross hack.
  • Figure out more about the ASC and EASC audio chips.

``When I have spare time:''

  • Resolve Duo problems: docked vs. undocked video, and two 5380- compatible SCSI controllers. Create a `duo' bus for attaching SCSI, Ethernet, video, etc. on Duodocks.
  • Integrate color support, and define a common interface for both NuBus-based and internal video. (This may not be feasible.)
