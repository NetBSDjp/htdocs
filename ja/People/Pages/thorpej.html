Jason Thorpe

This is me in front of the Rio Grande Gorge in northern New Mexico, during a camping trip with friends Lonnie, Yvonne, and Carolyn over the Thanksgiving holiday in 1997.

I've been using NetBSD for quite a while (mid 1993). I started with NetBSD 0.8, but I didn't really become heavily involved with NetBSD until NetBSD 0.9 was released. Prior to NetBSD, my UNIX experience was with 4.2BSD on a VAX, 4.3BSD on the HP 9000/300, and early versions of HP-UX, SunOS, and Dynix.

During the period between NetBSD 0.9 and NetBSD 1.0, I started hacking on NetBSD/hp300. At the time, I worked for, and was a student at, Oregon State University's Department of Computer Science. The department had gobs HP 9000/300-series systems, and we were already running Utah's 4.3BSD port on a bunch of these machines.

During the day I work for NASA Ames Research Center's Numerical Aerospace Simulation Facility, where I am a Network Systems Engineer in the Local Area Networks Group. I'm lucky enough to work on NetBSD as an integral part of my job; we are using NetBSD as a platform for network research and development. My friend and co-worker Kevin Lahey (another NetBSD developer) and I spend a lot of time working on the NetBSD network code, and are active participants in the Internet Engineering Task Force.

My two main NetBSD platforms are NetBSD/alpha and NetBSD/i386. In my lab and my tiny little office plot, I run a small cluster of NetBSD systems, including a SPARC, a couple of Alphas, a couple of PCs, a DECstation, a Power Macintosh, a couple of StrongARM Shark NCs, and a VAXstation. I also lug around an NEC Versa 6050MX laptop running NetBSD, and keep it connected to the world with Metricom Ricochet.

When I have spare brain cycles, I also play port-master for NetBSD/hp300. Hey, someone has to, and I still use an HP 9000/380 as my router and main server at home.

I have lots of things that consume the time I don't spend working. Several of them are outdoor activities, so they depend on the season somewhat.

Spring time is when I bring out the mountain bike, give it a tune-up, and start getting in shape for the summer biking season.

In the summer, I like to ride my bike in the beautiful preserves around the San Francisco Bay Area. I like to ride up in the City, too, and shoot for a trip across the Golden Gate Bridge and up into the Marin Headlands once a week. Summertime also sees me strap on rollerblades (a scary sight every afternoon!) and hiking boots. You're likely to catch me preparing for a camping trip around this time, as well. I'm also getting kayaking lessons from my friends Lonnie, Yvonne, and Randy. I'll get the hang of it one day.

I allocate the fall months to a bit more road riding; I like to get muddy, but trail damage is a serious problem when it gets really wet. (Are you getting the feeling that I like to bicycle yet?)

Winter time is when I pretty much put the bike away, and dig the skis out. I'm not really a down-hiller (I like the sensation of going down a hill fast on a bike a lot more than on skis), but I recently got into cross-country skiing.

When I'm not outdoors I like to play my guitar and/or listen to music, see non-mainstream movies (I just love the Castro Theater), see live music (it's not uncommon for me to somewhat impulsively travel to Portland, OR, my sort-of home town, to see a favorite band), or just drink beer and be merry with friends.

For kicks, you can check out FREAK-CAM, a little diversion I created to waste precious network bandwidth.

If you're ever in my neck of the woods (San Francisco Bay Area), drop me a line.
