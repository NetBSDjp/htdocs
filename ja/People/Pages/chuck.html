About Chuck

I have been a BSD user since 1985 when I started college (using 4.2BSD). Over the years I have progressed through 4.3BSD, MORE/bsd (from Mt. Xinu), SunOS 4.x, and finally 4.4BSD. I have been participating in the development of the free BSD operating systems (FreeBSD, NetBSD, and OpenBSD) since 1994. I have contributed sun4 support to the sparc port, integrated ATM networking into BSD, and written a replacement for the 4.4BSD virtual memory system (the UVM Virtual Memory System). I was also the port-master for the NetBSD mvme68k port from July 1995 (when it was first imported into the source tree) until February 1999. Recently I have been focusing my BSD-related hacking efforts on maintaining and upgrading UVM.

I am currently a Senior Technical Staff Member in the Distributed Systems Research department at AT&T Labs-Research in Florham Park, New Jeresy (about an hour west of New York City). Prior to joining AT&T I was a Computer Science doctoral student at Washington University in St. Louis Missouri (I completed my D.Sc. in August 1998). I also have a masters degree in Computer Science from Washington University, and a Bachelor of Electrical Engineering degree from University of Delaware (EG89).

Feel free to browse my home page, http://www.ccrc.wustl.edu/pub/chuck for more information on me and my interests.
