Experimental ports

Experimental ports are ports that have not yet been distributed as part of an official NetBSD release. They are available through the experimental NetBSD-current source distribution and some are additionally available via pre-compiled binary snapshots provided by the port maintainers from time to time.

We encourage you to test experimental ports, but not all of them will provide stable service. Those that are stable and ready will be available in the next currently scheduled release. Look at the individual pages for details, or ask on the port's corresponding mailing list.

Not yet integrated porting efforts

For architectures not listed above, no support is available as formal release or in the mainline experimental source distribution.

In most cases, people porting to a new machine wish to stay unknown to the public. This is a necessity, as answering "me too"-style requests just takes too much time. If you really think you can contribute to porting to a new machine (read: have started already; or know how to do it, but just don't want to duplicate efforts; or know lots about the internals of the machine which are not publicly known) you should contact the core team, who will provide contact to your co-strugglers.

However, for these machines, early testing setups are available:

  • Connectix VirtualPC, a software emulated Pentium machine running on PowerMAC machines. This should run NetBSD/i386 unchanged, but currently needs a set of patches developed by the University of Melbourne, Department of Computer Science.
  • NetBSD/pc98, a semi-PC compatible line of computers made by NEC. (Page is in Japanese).
