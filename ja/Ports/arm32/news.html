NetBSD/arm32 news archive

  • Recent news (on port homepage)
  • Commits affecting the port-arm32 portion of the source tree

Mar 15, 1999:
New snapshot available.

October 20th, 1998:
Port master may be even slower than normal to respond to e-mail due to moving to the SF Bay area over the next three weeks.

October 18th, 1998:
arm32 port now using newer IDE framework. Support for ARCIN v6 IDE card added.

October 17th, 1998:
Added Chaltech CATS board support to the tree, using nwscons console.

September 5th, 1998:
Separated hardware and software interrupt masks along with interrupt dispatching optimisations.

September 5th, 1998:
DC21285 'Footbridge' core logic, and EBSA285 architecture board support added.

September 3rd, 1998:
Updated web pages to look more like new NetBSD style. Nicer than previous ones!

September 3rd, 1998:
Mark committed another of Neil's MMU management improvements.

August 31st 1998:
Now allocating larger buffer cache for NetBSD/arm32. Much faster compiles and less disc thrashing!

August 28th 1998:
NetBSD/arm32 switched by default to UVM, Chuck Cranor's new VM system for NetBSD.
