NetBSD/arm32

About NetBSD/arm32

NetBSD/arm32 is a port of the OS to a variety of ARM- and StrongARM-powered computing platforms. The port has been a work in progress for the past four years, and is maintained by Mark Brinicombe.

There is really no such thing as `an arm32.' The first ARM processors (ARM2 and ARM3) were designed by Acorn, and had both 26 bit constraints and poor MMUs. Acorn later spun off ARM with Apple and VLSI. ARM's CPUs (6, 7, 8, 9 and StrongARM) were fully 32-bit and are supported by NetBSD.

Later, Digital licensed the ARM architecture to produce the StrongARM, a much faster CPU. This technology was then transferred to Intel, and the CPU is now the Intel StrongARM. The processor is being developed at a heightened pace, with SA-2 and SA-3 already in the pipeline.

NetBSD/arm32 News

Jan 05 2000: NetBSD-current snapshot
Based on 1999-12-23 sources, at: ftp://ftp.netbsd.org/pub/NetBSD/arch/arm32/snapshot/19991223/.

Oct 12, 1999:mpg123 package for arm32
Thanks to Simon Burge of the NetBSD Project, owners of Systems equipped with a StrongARM CPU can now enjoy playing MP3 files on their systems, too. The StrongARM CPU doesn't have a FPU, which was previously required by the mpg123 software to play sound. This limitation is now gone thanks to Simon's rewriting of the software to use fixed point (integer) math.

Sep 13, 1999:bpf fix for ie driver
Fixed Berkeley Packet Filter support in the 'ie' driver.

May 12, 1999: NetBSD-1.4 released
The NetBSD 1.4 release supports sixteen architectures, and was distributed free by Usenix to all 1999 ATC attendees.

Supported ARM and StrongARM CPUs logic

These are the CPUs and other components for which support exists in NetBSD/arm32. Listed here are video controllers, core logic, PCI bridges, floating point units etc.
  • ARM 600, 610
  • ARM 700, 710
  • ARM 810
  • ARM7500FE
  • ARM VIDC
  • ARM FPA10, FPA11
  • ARM IOMD
  • Intel DC21285 ("footbridge")
  • Intel StrongARM SA110

Supported computer systems

These are systems made by various computer manufacturers for which support as a whole exists. Systems for which support was added in NetBSD 1.4 are marked (1.4)
  • Acorn RiscPC600, RiscPC700.
  • Acorn StrongARM RiscPC, J233.
  • Acorn A7000, A7000+.
  • VLSI RC7500.
  • Digital DNARD ("Shark").
  • Chalice Technology CATS (1.4).
  • Intel EBSA-285 (1.4).

Earlier 'arm26' machines will be supported by the NetBSD/arm26 port.

Ongoing work

We are currently integrating the new machine-independant console, upgrading pmap, writing an XFree86 server for Acorn machines, generally cleaning up code and improving performance further.

People

Core processor support was written by Mark Brinicombe (most of it) and Neil Carson (some of libkern and pmap, floating point emulators and libraries).

Support for Acorn platforms was written by Mark Brinicombe (most if it) with help from Scott Stevens (various device drivers), Melvin Tang Richardson (VIDC console), Mike Pumford (EtherH driver) and Andrew McMurry (Acorn FileCore filing system).

Support for the Digital Shark was written by Dave Chaiken, Chris Demetriou, Jay Kistler, Todd Vierling and others. Support for the 21285 and CATS was written by Mark Brinicombe. Support for the VLSI RC7500 was contributed by Danny Tsen at VLSI.
