NetBSD/arm32

About NetBSD/arm32 

NetBSD/arm32 1.5.3 is the ninth formal release of NetBSD/arm32.

NetBSD/arm32 is a port of the OS to a variety of ARM- and StrongARM-powered computing platforms. The port has been a work in progress since 1994, and is maintained by Mark Brinicombe.

There is really no such thing as `an arm32.' The first ARM processors (ARM2 and ARM3) were designed by Acorn, and had both 26 bit constraints and poor MMUs. These processors are supported by NetBSD/acorn26 (formerly known as NetBSD/arm26). Acorn later spun off ARM with Apple and VLSI. ARM's CPUs (6, 7, 8, 9 and StrongARM) were fully 32-bit and are supported by NetBSD/arm32.

Later, Digital licensed the ARM architecture to produce the StrongARM, a much faster CPU. This technology was then transferred to Intel, and the CPU is now the Intel StrongARM.

2002-07-22: NetBSD 1.5.3 released
NetBSD 1.5.3 released with support for 20 architectures. More information is available in the 1.5.3 release announcement.

2001-12-19: Improvements in Acorn NC support
Ben Harris has modified the "ne at podulebus" driver to work around a bug in the MX98905 chip used on EtherI and EtherN cards, so they can now be used on NetBSD. He has also tweaked the vidcvideo driver to find screen modes correctly on the ARM7500. Between them, these allow an NC_WSCONS kernel to come up multi-user, with a proper display.

Ben is now working on getting X up and running on his Acorn NC.

More information on NetBSD/acorn32 can be found at the NetBSD/acorn32 port page.

Supported ARM and StrongARM CPUs 

These are the CPUs and other components for which support exists in NetBSD/arm32. Listed here are video controllers, core logic, PCI bridges, floating point units etc.

  • ARM600, ARM610
  • ARM700, ARM710
  • ARM810
  • ARM7500FE
  • ARM VIDC
  • ARM FPA10, FPA11
  • ARM IOMD
  • Intel DC21285 ("footbridge")
  • Intel SA-110


Supported Computer Systems 

The following systems were supported by NetBSD/arm32 in NetBSD 1.5, but will have their own separate ports in NetBSD 1.6. They're already separate in NetBSD-current.

  • Acorn Risc PC 600, Risc PC 700, StrongARM Risc PC, J233, A7000 and A7000+, now supported by NetBSD/acorn32.
  • Digital DNARD ("Shark"), now supported by NetBSD/shark.
  • Chalice Technology CATS, now supported by NetBSD/cats.

Earlier ARM-based Acorn machines are supported by NetBSD/acorn26 (formerly known as NetBSD/arm26). ARM-based Windows CE PDAs are supported by NetBSD/hpcarm. StrongARM based NetWinder machines are supported by NetBSD/netwinder.


People 

Core processor support was written by Mark Brinicombe (most of it) and Neil Carson (some of libkern and pmap, floating point emulators and libraries).

Support for Acorn platforms was written by Mark Brinicombe (most if it) with help from Scott Stevens (various device drivers), Melvin Tang Richardson (VIDC console), Mike Pumford (EtherH driver) and Andrew McMurry (Acorn FileCore filing system).

Support for the Digital Shark was written by Dave Chaiken, Chris Demetriou, Jay Kistler, Todd Vierling and others. Support for the 21285 and CATS was written by Mark Brinicombe. Support for the VLSI RC7500 was contributed by Danny Tsen at VLSI.


Ongoing Work 

We are currently integrating the new machine-independant console, upgrading pmap, writing an XFree86 server for Acorn machines, generally cleaning up code and improving performance further.
