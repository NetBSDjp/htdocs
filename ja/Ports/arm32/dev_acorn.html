NetBSD/arm32 devices supported on Acorn machines

On-board hardware

Most standard Acorn hardware is catered for. This includes VIDC (video console), IOMD (general purpose IO), serial port, parallel port, mouse (both quadrature and PS/2), internal IDE interface, floppy disc and keyboard.
Ethernet interfaces
NetBSD/arm32 supports all those interfaces with machine-independent podulebus drivers, and also:

RISC OS driver name Manufacturer NetBSD driver name
Ether1Acornie0
EtherH NICI-cubedne0
EtherM NICANTne0

EtherH and EtherM NIC drivers are presently the best performers.

IO cards
In addition to those podules with machine-independent drivers, the following IO cards are supported:

  • Acorn SCSI
  • Cumana SCSI I (Cumana II is not currently working)
  • Alsystems Powertec SCSI II (maybe/apparently SCSI III)
  • MCS Connect32
  • Morley uncached SCSI
  • Yellowstone RapIDE (issue 2 - approximately 15% faster than internal IDE)
  • Simtec low-cost IDE (approximately the same speed as internal IDE)
  • ICS IDE Arcin v5 and v6

The speed of the SCSI drivers is presently relatively poor, whereas the IDE drivers perform well.
