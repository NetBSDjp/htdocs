NetBSD/amd64

NetBSD/amd64 is a port to the AMD64 family of processors. It was first committed to the NetBSD source tree as NetBSD/x86_64 on june 19th, 2001. It was renamed to NetBSD/amd64 on april 27th, 2003.

The original work to do this port was done by Frank van der Linden at Wasabi Systems, assisted by AMD, who provided the simulator (Simics VirtuHammer) and later pre-release hardware.

The port is fully functional. It has been tested on single-CPU and MP Opteron configurations. It will be a completely supported platform in the next NetBSD release. Because it needs a newer gcc version than currently is present in the NetBSD source tree, automatic snapshots are not built. If you want to obtain a snapshot of NetBSD-current for AMD64, contact the port maintainer.

If you want to stay informed about, and/or discuss things related to NetBSD/amd64, join the NetBSD/amd64 mailing list.

Frank van der Linden is the maintainer of NetBSD/amd64.
