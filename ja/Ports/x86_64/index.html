NetBSD/x86_64

NetBSD/x86_64 is a port to AMD's upcoming 64 bit architecture. It was committed to the NetBSD source tree on june 19th, 2001. It was originally developed on the Simics Virtuhammer simulator, but has meanwhile been tested on actual x86-64 hardware, on which it runs well.

The work to do this port was done by Frank van der Linden at Wasabi Systems, assisted by AMD, who provided the simulator and the hardware.

The port is fully functional, and will be a completely supported platform in the next NetBSD release. Because it needs a newer gcc version than currently is present in the NetBSD source tree, and a newer XFree86 version than is released with NetBSD, automatic snapshots are not built. If you want to obtain a snapshot of NetBSD-current for x86-64, contact the port maintainer.

Frank van der Linden is the maintainer of NetBSD/x86_64.
