NetBSD/sun2

About NetBSD/sun2 

NetBSD/sun2 is the port of NetBSD to the Sun Microsystems sun2 series of computers, which are based on the Motorola 68010 CPU with a Sun-designed custom MMU. Sun sold these computers as both servers and desktop workstations from the early to mid 1980's. They were superceded by the sun3 systems, which are supported by NetBSD as NetBSD/sun3.

Matt Fredette is the maintainer of NetBSD/sun2.

Its current status is that of an experimental port.

For frequently asked questions and links to Sun hardware information, see the NetBSD/sun2 FAQ.

Supported System Models 

While no official snapshots or releases of NetBSD/sun2 have been made, initial support is planned for the following systems:

  • Sun2 Multibus machines:
    • Sun 2/120
    • Sun 2/170


Supported Hardware 

While no official snapshots or releases of NetBSD/sun2 have been made, initial support is planned for the following hardware:

  • 1 bpp framebuffer, Multibus card (bwtwo)
  • 10 Mbps Intel 82586 ethernet, Multibus card (ie)
  • 5 MB/s Sun-2 SCSI controller, Multibus card (Interrupt driven and polled DMA) (sc)
  • All on-board and optional serial ports
  • Most input devices (keyboards, mice, trackballs)


System Models Not Supported 

While no official snapshots or releases of NetBSD/sun2 have been made, when any are made, the following hardware will not be supported initially:

  • Sun2 VME machines:
    • Sun 2/50
    • Sun 2/130
    • Sun 2/160
