NetBSD/acorn32

About NetBSD/acorn32 

NetBSD/acorn32 is due to have its first formal release in 1.6 and is a port to ARM- and StrongARM-powered Acorn RiscPC compatible machines running on ARM6 and newer and is maintained by Reinoud Zandijk. Support for the Acorns for NetBSD 1.5.x is found in NetBSD/arm32.

The port saw the first light as RiscBSD in 1994 and was later transformed to NetBSD/arm32. There is really no such thing as `an arm32.' The first ARM processors (ARM2 and ARM3) were designed by Acorn, and had both 26 bit constraints and poor MMUs. These processors are supported by NetBSD/arm26. Acorn later spun off ARM with Apple and VLSI. ARM's CPUs (6, 7, 8, 9 and StrongARM) are fully 32-bit and are supported by the NetBSD/arm32 derived ports.

Later, Digital licensed the ARM architecture to produce the StrongARM, a much faster CPU. This technology was then transferred to Intel, and this CPU is now known as the Intel StrongARM and its successor the Intel Xscale (StrongARM-II).


NetBSD/acorn32 News 

2001-11-20: NetBSD/acorn32 created
NetBSD/acorn32 split off from NetBSD/arm32 and is now maintained by Reinoud Zandijk as part of the ARM team.

2001-04-02: Hardware scrolling for RiscPC/A7000/NC wscons
Reinoud Zandijk has committed code for hardware scrolling for the RiscPC and compatibles. This new code has been tested in different screen resolutions and depths, and its all worked ok so far. It even appears to run faster than the original vidcconsole code. More details are available in Reinoud's announcement in the port-arm32 mail archive.

Supported ARM and StrongARM CPUs 

These are the CPUs and other components for which support exists in NetBSD/arm32. Listed here are video controllers, core logic, PCI bridges, floating point units etc.

  • ARM600, ARM610
  • ARM700, ARM710
  • ARM810
  • ARM7500FE
  • ARM VIDC
  • ARM FPA10, FPA11
  • ARM IOMD
  • Intel SA-110


Supported Computer Systems 

These are systems made by various computer manufacturers for which support as a whole exists.

  • Acorn Risc PC 600, Risc PC 700
  • Acorn StrongARM Risc PC, J233
  • Acorn A7000, A7000+
  • Acorn network computers (partial as yet)

The following systems used to be also supported by NetBSD/arm32, in NetBSD 1.5 but will have their own separate ports in NetBSD 1.6. They're already separate in NetBSD-current.

  • Digital DNARD ("Shark"), now supported by NetBSD/dnard.
  • Chalice Technology CATS, now supported by NetBSD/cats.

Earlier ARM-based Acorn machines are supported by NetBSD/arm26. ARM-based Windows CE PDAs are supported by NetBSD/hpcarm. StrongARM based NetWinder machines are supported by NetBSD/netwinder.


People 

Core processor support was written by Mark Brinicombe (most of it) and Neil Carson (some of libkern and pmap, floating point emulators and libraries).

Support for Acorn platforms was written by Mark Brinicombe (most if it) with help from Scott Stevens (various device drivers), Melvin Tang Richardson (VIDC console), Mike Pumford (EtherH driver) and Andrew McMurry (Acorn FileCore filing system).
