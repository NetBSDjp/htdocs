Funding and contributing to NetBSD

NetBSD is a non-profit volunteer organization that produces open source software. This means that the Project has no regular source of income, instead relying on generous donations from third parties.

Funding for the NetBSD Project comes from a variety of sources. Organizations and individuals interested in NetBSD contribute money, hardware and rack space to support important Project services, and to enable developers to support new hardware platforms and peripherals.

In addition, some organizations and individuals contribute money and staff for promotional events such as trade shows. Some trade show organizers also provide free or low-cost space.

It's important to understand that every donation matters. Without servers and rack space at colocation facilities, we would not be able to provide high-quality centralized services such as revision control and mailing lists to our developers and users. Without donations of development hardware, we would not be able to support the huge range of platforms and peripherals we do today. Without money and space to do promotional work, we would not be able to present our work to other people and acquire new developers and users. Each of these functions is important to the ongoing health of the Project.

So, whether you're a Fortune 100 company shipping NetBSD in a product, a company running your web server on NetBSD, a home user with NetBSD on your desktop, or just someone who thinks NetBSD is cool — or all four — please consider donating something to the Project.

How you can help

The easiest way to donate is of course monetarily. For other ideas, read more about how an organization or individual can contribute.

Thanks to our past and ongoing contributors

We would like to thank the innumerable people who have contributed time, code, documentation, financial or material aid to the project, without whom NetBSD could not exist in the form it does today.
