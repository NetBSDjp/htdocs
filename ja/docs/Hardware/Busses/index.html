Busses

Listed here are just some of the busses that NetBSD users and hackers might find interesting. If a listed bus doesn't have a link, that means we don't currently have any interesting material on the subject. If you find a link that could be handy, please let us know!

  • ADB - Apple Desktop Bus
  • ATA - AT Attachment (evolution of ISA/IDE)
  • DEBI - Main interface card bus on Acorn RiscPC and similar machines.
  • EISA - Extended Industry Standard Architecture
  • HP-IB - Hewlett-Packard Interface Bus
  • I2C - Philips I2C
  • IEEE1394 - Firewire, i.Link
  • ISA - Industry Standard Architecture
  • Massbus - DEC, used in VAX 700 series machines
  • MCA - MicroChannel Architecture
  • NuBus - Apple's NuBus
  • PCI - Peripheral Component Interconnect
  • PDS - Apple's 'Processor-Direct Slot'
  • Q-bus - Used in PDP, VAX, and some DECstation boxes
  • SCSI - Small Computer System Interface
  • Sbus - Sun Sbus
  • SDI - Standard Drive Interconnect
  • TurboChannel - DEC, used in VAXstations, DECstations, and Alphas
  • Unibus - DEC, used in VAX 700 series machines
  • USB - Universal Serial Bus.
  • VAXBI - DEC, used in VAX 6000 and 8000 series
  • VL-Bus - VESA Local Bus (evolution of ISA, initially for faster video)
  • VME - VMEbus
  • XMI - DEC, used in VAX 6000, 7000, 9000, and 10000 series
  • Zorro - Commodore's Amiga bus.
